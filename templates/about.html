My name is Omar Chaarawi and I am an aspiring software developer and data scientist/engineer. I live in Boulder, Colorado where I work as an arborist as I pursue a computer science education at University of Colorado Boulder. I first became interested in computer science during an internship at MIT while I was earning my bachelor degree in chemistry at Ithaca College. I had the opportunity to work in a computational chemistry lab as an undergraduate research assistant. I was building models of molecules and using physical chemistry computations to better understand molecular systems. The programming aspects stimulated me in a way chemistry had not. After I finished my chemistry degree I enrolled in classes at CU Boulder and started a track toward a computer science education.

My first official computer science class was Harvard's CS50: Intro to Computer Science. While taking CS50 there was a project developing a small webpage for conducting mock trades. The site made use of API's, used to pull stock data in real time and databases which were used to maintain a record of user data. This was my initial exposure to building a website from scratch using a Flask framework. Omarchaarawi.com is my personal website that I have built to represent some of my projects. It has also enhanced my website development skills. I have additional programming projects in various languages listed under the projects tab.

By nature, programming is time consuming; however, I am able to maintain balance while I pursue this challenging field. I am a runner, climber, backcountry skier and mountain biker. I enjoy the labor of these sports much like the labor of programming. The problem solving and challenge has been rewarding for me, and I am eager to start this next phase of my career.
